## Description

```
Responding to a crucial need to support the local tourism economy, especially highlighted during the COVID-19 pandemic's challenges, the Puerto Rico Tourism Company launched the "Una Isla, 78 Destinos," a campaign in 2021 featuring a tourism passport to boost local exploration across Puerto Rico's 78 municipalities. The physical passport, initially a simple promotional tool, unexpectedly evolved into a big sensation, revealing a public appetite for interactive and competitive exploration. But the passport had its drawbacks, as it was hard to carry around, it couldn’t be updated with the latest attractions and events and there were times when there wasn’t enough supply to meet demand.

In 2023, the Puerto Rico Tourism Company unveiled "Passport to Adventure," transforming the concept of local tourism into a digital, interactive experience. This initiative was designed in response to the desire for a more dynamic way to discover Puerto Rico, moving beyond traditional exploration methods to a digital platform that prioritizes user experience (UX) and interface design (UI) on mobile devices. By focusing on seamless navigation and visual appeal, "Passport to Adventure" invites users on a gamified journey through Puerto Rico's rich landscapes and cultural sites.

"Passport to Adventure” introduces a groundbreaking blend of geolocation and RFID technologies, setting a new standard in digital tourism experiences. The platform offers a unique, user-centered approach to exploring Puerto Rico, ensuring each interaction is not only intuitive but also enriching.

What truly sets this initiative apart is its innovative blend of digital interaction and real-world exploration that creates a unique, seamless, immersive User Experience, setting a new standard in the tourism sector. It not only encourages physical engagement with the island’s many wonders, but also marks a significant breakthrough in how destinations can merge technology with tangible exploration to enhance tourist engagement and satisfaction. 

Its groundbreaking use of geolocation technologies, coupled with RFID-enabled signage, facilitated physical check-ins at various locations. Using the methodology and frameworks of Design Thinking, we optimized the platform based on user-centric and accessible design.
```
