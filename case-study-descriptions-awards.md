Title: REUSABLES \- SuperMax \- de la Cruz Puerto Rico \- Video Case Study

Client: SuperMax

Description: This project addresses the significant issue of food waste caused by consumer misconceptions regarding "use by" dates. SuperMax, a leading online grocery store in Puerto Rico, introduced an educational program called REUSABLES. By utilizing augmented reality filters on Instagram and their website, the program shows users how to repurpose products after their expiration date rather than discarding them, providing life hacks that give these items a second life.  
The "Reusables" campaign, created for the Puerto Rican supermarket chain SuperMax by the agency De La Cruz / Ogilvy, has received several prestigious international awards for its innovative approach to reducing food waste through technology.   
Award Wins  
The campaign was highly successful at the FIAP Awards 2023 (Ibero-American Advertising Festival), winning three Bronze awards:  
Bronze in the Best Sustainability Campaign category.  
Bronze in the Mobile Innovation category.  
Bronze in the Best Program Launch Strategy category.   
Additionally, the campaign was recognized as a Cannes Contender in 2023, representing one of the top creative highlights for the Ogilvy network at the Cannes Lions International Festival of Creativity.   
Campaign Concept  
The project addressed the issue of food waste by using Augmented Reality (AR) and machine learning to "hack" expiration dates.   
The "Use For" Shift: It reframed traditional "Use By" labels into "Use For" ideas.  
Technology: Shoppers could scan products nearing their expiration date to receive recipes and tips on how to reuse the items (e.g., using ketchup to clean brass or turning old milk into a face mask) rather than throwing them away.

"Car Parts for Life" is an award-winning social impact campaign from Puerto Rico that uses the metaphor of donated car parts to promote organ donation. Created by the agency de la Cruz (Ogilvy) for the Fundación Stefano Steenbakkers Betancourt, it has received numerous prestigious accolades in 2024 and 2025\.
